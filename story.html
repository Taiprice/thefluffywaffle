The woman behind the waffle

Good food is therapy.

The story of how a lifelong love of feeding people led to a waffle iron, a farmers market booth, and a whole lot of joy.

👩🏽‍🍳

Michelle

Founder & Head Waffle Maker

Her story

From chocolate chip cookies
to culinary obsession.

The beginning

People love dessert.

Michelle's obsession with the culinary world began in her early twenties during college with a simple discovery: people love dessert. Her earliest attempts involved chocolate chip cookies as well as peanut butter cookies, but it was the joy on people's faces as they devoured them that truly sparked her culinary ambition.

The spark becomes a flame

A self-taught culinary education.

When the Food Network debuted, a spark became a flame. As a self-taught cook, Michelle used television and a growing collection of cookbooks as her culinary academy, constantly experimenting with advanced techniques and unfamiliar ingredients. By her late twenties, she had officially taken the reins of the family's annual Thanksgiving feast — ambitiously preparing the turkey and every single side from scratch.

Preserving the past

Recipes lost to time.

As her own family grew, Michelle felt a deep pull toward her roots. She set out to uncover old family recipes, only to find a heartbreaking reality: many were lost to time, scribbled on fading 3x5 index cards, or buried with generations who had passed on. Determined to safeguard these culinary secrets for the future, she began recreating them and compiling them into cookbooks. Today, she has created six distinct editions — including a specialized collection for camping and another dedicated entirely to waffles.

A lifelong foodie

Every Michelin star. Every James Beard table.

Driven by a love for exceptional food, Michelle and her husband have spent years exploring every Michelin-starred, James Beard-recognized, and critically acclaimed restaurant in the local area. A proud, lifelong foodie, she constantly seeks out fine gastronomic adventures for inspiration.

The missing piece

It was right in front of her all along.

While she frequently dreamed of launching a brick-and-mortar restaurant or a food truck, the timing and logistics never quite aligned. The missing piece of the puzzle finally clicked during a weekend stroll through a local community market. Surrounded by the energy of local vendors and food lovers, she realized the perfect venue had been right in front of her all along. She created a website and an Instagram account — and just like that, the story continued... good food is therapy.

📚

Six cookbooks and counting.

Michelle has authored six distinct cookbook editions, preserving family recipes for future generations. Her collection includes a specialized camping cookbook and a volume dedicated entirely to waffles — the same recipes that inspired The Fluffy Waffle.

"Good food is therapy."
— Michelle, Founder of The Fluffy Waffle
